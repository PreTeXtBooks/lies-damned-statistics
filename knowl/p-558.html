In Example Example 4.3.18, we needed to compute \(P(X>72)\) where \(X\) was known to be \(N(69, 2.8)\text{.}\) Is 72 one of the numbers for which we should be looking, to use the Rule? Well, it’s greater than \(\mu_X=69\text{,}\) so we could hope that it was \(\mu_X+\sigma_X\text{,}\) \(\mu_X+2\sigma_X\text{,}\) or \(\mu_X+3\sigma_X\text{.}\) But values are
\begin{align*} \mu_X+\sigma_X&=69+2.8=71.8,\\ \mu_X+2\sigma_X&=69+5.6=74.6, \text{ and}\\ \mu_X+3\sigma_X&=69+8.4=77.4, \end{align*}
none of which is what we need.
