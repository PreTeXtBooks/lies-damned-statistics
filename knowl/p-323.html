Extrapolation is considered a bad, or at least risky, practice. The idea is that we used the evidence in the dataset \(\{(x_1,y_1), \dots , (x_n,y_n)\}\) to build the LSRL, but, by definition, all of this data lies in the interval on the \(x\)-axis from \(x_{min}\) to \(x_{max}\text{.}\) There is literally no evidence from this dataset about what the relationship between our chosen explanatory and response variables will be for \(x\) outside of this interval. So in the absence of strong reasons to believe that the precise linear relationship described by the LSRL will continue for more \(x\)â€™s, we should not assume that it does, and therefore we should not use the LSRL equation to guess values by extrapolation.
