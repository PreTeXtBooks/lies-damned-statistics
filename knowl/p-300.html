Working with the statistics students’ homework and total course points data from Example Example 3.1.3, suppose the gradebook of the course instructor was somewhat corrupted and the instructor lost the final course points of the student Janet. If Janet’s homework points of 77 were not in the corrupted part of the gradebook, the instructor might use interpolation to guess what Janet’s total course point probably were. To do this, the instructor would have plugged in \(x=77\) into the equation of the LSRL, \(\widehat{y}=mx+b\) to get the estimated total course points of \(.754\cdot77+26.976=85.034\text{.}\)
