Imagine you live on a street with a stop light at both ends of the block. You watch cars driving down the street and notice which ones have to stop at the \(1^{\text{st}}\) and/or \(2^{\text{nd}}\) light (or none). After counting cars and stops for a year, you have seen what a very large number — call it \(N\) — of cars did. Now imagine you decide to think about the experiment “pick a car on this street from the last year at random and notice at which light or lights it has to stop.”
