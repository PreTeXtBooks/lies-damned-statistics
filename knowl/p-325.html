Using again the statistics students’ homework and total course points data from Example Example 3.1.3, suppose the course instructor wanted to predict what would be the total course points for a student who had earned a perfect \(100\) points on their homework. Plugging into the LSRL, this would have yielded a guess of \(.754\cdot100+26.976=102.376\text{.}\) Of course, this would have been impossible, since the maximum possible total course score was \(100\text{.}\) Moreover, making this guess is an example of extrapolation, since the \(x\) value of \(100\) is beyond the largest \(x\) value of \(x_{max}=92\) in the dataset. Therefore we should not rely on this guess — as makes sense, since it is invalid by virtue of being larger than \(100\text{.}\)
