In particular, if \(x_2-x_1=1\text{,}\) then \(m=\frac{y_2-y_1}{1}=y_2-y_1\) — so if you look at how much the line goes up in each step of one unit to the right, that number will be the slope \(m\) (and if it goes down, the slope \(m\) will simply be negative). In other words, the slope answers the question “for each step to the right, how much does the line increase (or decrease)?”
