The idea of interpolation is that we think the LSRL describes as well as possible the relationship between the independent and dependent variables, so that if we have a new \(x\) value, we’ll use the LSRL equation to predict what would be our best guess of what would be the corresponding \(y\text{.}\) Note we might have a new value of \(x\) because we simply lost part of our dataset and are trying to fill it in as best we can. Another reason might be that a new individual came along whose value of the independent variable, \(x_0\text{,}\) was typical of the rest of the dataset — so the the very least \(x_{min}\le x_0\le x_{max}\) — and we want to guess what will be the value of the dependent variable for this individual before we measure it. (Or maybe we cannot measure it for some reason.)
